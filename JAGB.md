JAGB — J-band Asymptotic Giant Branch


WHAT IT IS

Carbon-rich AGB stars in the J band cluster at a remarkably constant
absolute magnitude, M_J ≈ –6.2 ± 0.05 mag. Unlike Cepheids and TRGB
the standard candle here is a population mode, not a strict luminosity
boundary: the C/O ratio in carbon stars limits the upper J-band
brightness through a thermal-pulse / dust-formation feedback loop.
Calibrated against LMC, SMC, and a handful of Milky Way carbon stars,
JAGB delivers ~3-5 % distances out to several Mpc with JWST or
ground-based NIR photometry, and sits above TRGB in luminosity (so
JWST can probe it well past where HST measures the tip).


ROLE IN H0DN

JAGB is excluded from the H0DN baseline (host_data.dat strips rows
containing 'jagb'). Variant V01 ("Baseline + JAGB") re-includes them
by removing 'jagb' from the exclusion list, which lets the JAGB
distance scale weight the network. This is one of the variants that
tests methodological robustness — does adding a relatively recent
NIR-based primary indicator nudge the consensus H0?
