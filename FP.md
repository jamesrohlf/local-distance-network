FP — Fundamental Plane (of elliptical galaxies)


WHAT IT IS

Elliptical galaxies obey a tight 3-parameter scaling relation: their
effective radius R_e, central velocity dispersion σ, and mean surface
brightness ⟨I⟩_e all lie on a 2D plane in log space, R_e ∝ σ^a
⟨I⟩_e^b. Because R_e is an angular size at known distance, the FP
gives a relative distance once a and b and a zero-point are
calibrated. With dispersions and surface photometry of large
elliptical samples (DESI BGS, 6dFGS) it reaches several hundred Mpc.
Distance precision is modest per-galaxy (~25 %) but averages down
over thousands of galaxies into a competitive H0 measurement.


ROLE IN H0DN

The FP is not in the baseline; it is added in variant V03
("Baseline + DESI FP calibrated to Coma"), which uses coma_sn.dat
plus an explicit sigma_mag_fp parameter to bring DESI's FP H0
estimate into the network. It provides an independent check on the
high-end of the distance scale (Coma cluster and out) using a
completely different astrophysical mechanism than supernovae or
SBF. The DESI route in this figure is exactly this variant.
