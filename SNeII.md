SNe II — Type II (core-collapse) Supernovae


WHAT IT IS

The explosions of massive (> 8 M⊙) hydrogen-rich stars at the end of
their lives. Two distinct distance methods exist:

  • Standardized Candle Method (SCM): an empirical, SN-Ia-like
    relation between expansion velocity at +50 days, color, and
    plateau magnitude.

  • Expanding Photosphere Method (EPM): a quasi-physical method that
    compares the photospheric angular size (from photometry +
    atmospheric models) to the expansion velocity (from line widths)
    to extract a true Baade–Wesselink-style distance. EPM is the
    most independent supernova distance method available.

Per-supernova precision is poorer than SNe Ia (~15 %) but the
methodology is genuinely independent of the SN Ia ladder.


ROLE IN H0DN

SNe II are excluded from the baseline. Two variants add them
differently: V04 ("Baseline + empirically calibrated SNe II") adds
sn2_calib.dat + sn2_hf.dat; V05 ("Baseline + SNe II with EPM") adds
epm.dat with vcorr_epm = 2M++. Their inclusion provides an
independent check on the SN Ia–dominated long-range arm. The Pop-I
route (de Jaeger et al. 2022) and adh0cc route (Vogl et al. 2025) in
this figure are external published H0 estimates derived primarily
from SN II SCM and EPM methods respectively.
