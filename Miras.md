Miras — Mira Variables (long-period AGB pulsators)


WHAT IT IS

Miras are oxygen-rich AGB stars pulsating in the fundamental mode with
periods of 100-1000 days and visual amplitudes of 2.5+ magnitudes.
Like Cepheids they obey a period-luminosity relation, sharpest in the
NIR where M_K depends on log(P) with intrinsic scatter ~0.15 mag.
Calibrated against LMC and ω Cen Miras, they reach SNe Ia hosts at
several tens of Mpc. Their advantage is age: Miras are old (~1-10
Gyr), so they exist in elliptical galaxies that lack Cepheids,
including key SN Ia hosts. JWST opens up routine Mira observations
at distances comparable to the SH0ES Cepheid sample.


ROLE IN H0DN

Like JAGB, Miras are excluded from the baseline (host_data.dat strips
rows containing 'mira'). Variant V02 ("Baseline + Miras") removes
'mira' from the exclusion list, letting Mira-based host distances
contribute alongside Cepheids and TRGB. The V01/V02 pair (Baseline +
JAGB, Baseline + Miras) tests whether adding either of the new IR
secondary distance scales perturbs the consensus H0 — a check on
methodological diversity of the network.
