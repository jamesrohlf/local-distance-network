TF — Tully–Fisher relation


WHAT IT IS

In spiral galaxies, the rotation speed (measured from HI 21-cm line
widths or stellar kinematics) correlates tightly with absolute
luminosity: brighter spirals rotate faster, with M ∝ –a·log(W),
where W is the line width corrected for inclination. The relation
follows from virial-like scaling between mass and luminosity in disk
galaxies. Calibrated against Cepheid or TRGB hosts, TF delivers
~15-20 % distances per galaxy and reaches well into the Hubble flow
when applied to large samples. Cosmicflows-4 has compiled ~10⁴ TF
distances suitable for H0 work.


ROLE IN H0DN

TF is excluded from the baseline. Variant V06 ("Baseline +
Tully-Fisher") adds it via tf_calib.dat (calibrator galaxies) and
tf_hf.dat (Hubble-flow sample), with vcorr 2M++ for peculiar-velocity
correction. This is the variant labeled "Baseline + TF" in this
figure (id v06). The CF4 route uses the same TF data but combined
into its own published H0 estimate rather than within the H0DN
network framework.
