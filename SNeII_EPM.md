SNe II EPM — Expanding Photosphere Method


WHAT IT IS

EPM is a Baade–Wesselink-style distance technique applied to Type II
supernovae during their photospheric phase. Spectroscopy gives the
photospheric expansion velocity from absorption-line minima.
Photometry gives the integrated flux. Stellar-atmosphere models
predict the relationship between observed flux and photospheric
angular size. Combining these turns the supernova into its own
distance ruler — the photosphere expands at a known velocity, so
its angular size at later epochs is a known multiple of its earlier
angular size, and the absolute size sets the distance. EPM bypasses
the entire calibration ladder: no anchors, no Cepheids, no SNe Ia.


ROLE IN H0DN

EPM is excluded from the baseline. Variant V05 ("Baseline + SNe II
with EPM") adds epm.dat with peculiar-velocity correction
vcorr_epm = 2M++. EPM provides one of the very few completely
independent paths to the Hubble constant — its inclusion as a check
on the consensus is methodologically powerful even if its per-SN
precision is modest. The adh0cc route in this figure (Vogl et al.
2025) is a published EPM-only H0 estimate for context.
